### Step 13 — PASS (with a recorded limitation)
- **Proper termination clears the session**: a client that called `transport.terminateSession()` (explicit DELETE) was removed from the audit (active count 3→2; the server's `transport.onclose` → `auditLog.noteSessionClose` fired). This is step 13's specified behavior ("once all transports close, the indicator hides").
- **Known limitation (recorded, not blocking)**: a client that disconnects WITHOUT a DELETE — abrupt process death, or the SDK's `client.close()` which only tears down locally (it does NOT DELETE; `terminateSession()` is a separate call) — leaves a stale "connected" entry. The SDK server transport treats a dropped SSE stream as resumable, so such sessions linger until a DELETE or app restart (`stop()` cascades `transport.close()` → `onclose`). Consistent with DD6's "connected reflects transport lifecycle (a session lingers until its transport closes)", but a crashed/non-terminating client can leave the indicator stale. See Orchestrator Notes for the disposition + fast-follow candidate.

## Step Results

### Step 1 — PASS (precondition probe)
`tools/list` includes `getChromeTarget`; `getChromeTarget()` → `{wcId:1, kind:'chrome', url:…}`
(numeric). Baseline captured; baseline tab count read via `readAxTree`.

### Step 2 — PASS (few tabs expand, no scroll)
- **Raw (4 tabs):** widths `[240,240,240,240]`; `#tabs` `scrollWidth == clientWidth` (972 == 972)
  → **no overflow**; every in-view tab has favicon + close within its bounds.
- **Verdict:** tabs expand to share width (comfortably wide, capped ~240), no horizontal scroll.

### Step 3 — PASS (many tabs shrink, still visible, no scroll)
- **Raw (10 tabs):** widths all `110` (shrank from 240); `scrollWidth == clientWidth`
  (1132 == 1132) → **no overflow**; all 10 in-view tabs keep favicon + close **within** their
  bounds (titles ellipsize). Not the old fixed-120–220px-with-always-on-scrollbar behavior.
- **Verdict:** tabs shrink to share the width, favicon/close stay visible, no scrollbar. `[a11y]`

### Step 4 — PASS (scroll is last-resort)
- **Raw (13 tabs):** tabs hit the **floor 88px**; `scrollWidth 1192 > clientWidth 1132`
  → **overflow / horizontal scroll affordance appears** only now; favicon + close still render
  within each in-view tab at the floor.
- **Verdict:** scroll onset only after the floor cannot fit all tabs — last-resort fallback.

### Step 5 — PASS (pointer-close defers reflow)
- **Raw (above-floor 9-tab regime @122px):** `click(1, closeX=616, closeY=23)` on the middle
  tab's close button (pointer ends over the strip). Count **9 → 8**; remaining widths **frozen at
  122** (`leftWidthsUnchanged=true`, `widthsFrozen=true` — no re-expand); the tab that had been
  immediately to the right slid left so it is **now under the same close coordinate**
  (`slidUnderCoord` == the recorded right-neighbor title).
- **Verdict:** deferred reflow — remaining tabs keep their widths, the right neighbor slides into
  the closed slot under the unmoved cursor. (`s6-before-mouseleave.png` shows the frozen narrow
  tabs not filling the strip.)

### Step 6 — PASS (second freeze, then mouseleave re-expand)
- **Raw:** a second pointer-close (`click` on the close now under the coordinate) again removes a
  tab with **widths frozen at 122** (`widthFrozenAfterClose=true`). Then `click(1, 700, 500)`
  outside `#tabstrip` (mid web-content) induces `mouseleave` → widths **re-expand 122 → 158**
  (`reExpandedOnLeave=true`), trailing gap gone.
- **Verdict:** the deferred freeze holds through pointer-close and releases only once the pointer
  leaves the strip. (`s6-after-mouseleave.png` shows the same tabs re-expanded to fill the strip.)
  *Caveat per spec:* the re-expand fires on the outside-click that also carries the `mouseleave`;
  the two cannot be pixel-separated. The **load-bearing** evidence is that the tab removal did
  **not** re-expand while the pointer stayed over the strip (frozen at 122 across two closes) and
  did re-expand once it left — the deferral is demonstrated regardless of the exact trigger edge.

### Step 7 — PASS (maximize/restore state read — DD7)
- **Raw:** `#win-max` before = `data-state="normal"`, `aria-label="Maximize"`, `outerWidth=1432`.
  `click` → `data-state="maximized"`, `aria-label="Restore"`, `outerWidth=2560` (window physically
  maximized to full screen width). `click` again → `data-state="normal"`, `aria-label="Maximize"`,
  `outerWidth=1432` (restored).
- **Verdict:** the button's observable state (`data-state` + accessible name) flips to Restore on
  maximize and back to Maximize on restore, tracking **real** window state — the DD7 read path
  works, and `win.maximize()` **does** reliably maximize/restore on this WSLg compositor (better
  than the spec's `needs-human-recheck` caveat feared). `[a11y]`

### Step 8 — PASS (keyboard-close reflows immediately — contrast with Step 5)
- **Raw (fresh clean window, above-floor 9-tab regime @122px):** focus a tab via anchor click +
  ShiftTab (`activeElement` role `tab`), last pointer click on the **address bar** (NOT over the
  strip); `pressKey(1,'Delete')` → count **9 → 8**, width **122 → 138** immediately
  (`reflowedImmediately=true`) with **no** `mouseleave` needed.
- **Verdict:** keyboard-close re-expands the strip immediately — the deferred-resize freeze applies
  only to pointer-close while the pointer is over the strip, not to keyboard close.

Responsive sizing (expand → shrink → floor → scroll), the pointer-close deferred-reflow-then-
mouseleave-re-expand contract, its keyboard-close immediate-reflow contrast, and the DD7

no `LIKELY-REGRESSION` findings. Only apparatus notes: the WSLg maximize `innerWidth` artifact

mouseleave/outside-click coincidence in Step 6 (deferral still unambiguously demonstrated by the

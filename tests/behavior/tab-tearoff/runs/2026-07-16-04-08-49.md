## Erratum 1 (LOAD-BEARING) — the recorder MULTIPLIES; the arm never disconnects

The spec said *"re-arm the per-record recorder"* at rows 6/7/8/9 and **never said to
`disconnect()` the prior observer**. A `MutationObserver` on a live node is kept alive **by
the registration**, and the callback's `window.__gfAnn.push(...)` is a **dynamic** lookup —
so every prior observer keeps pushing into whatever array the name now binds.
**N arms ⇒ N observers ⇒ N pushes per single announcement.** C1 reached **×4** by step 8.

**Rows 4 and 6 read `EXACTLY ['…']` — TRUE ONLY BY LUCK**: each was the first arm on its own
chrome. Rows 7/8/9 read the same string N times, so the literal "exactly" wording is **wrong
as written** for any row after the first arm on a given chrome.

**Proved to be the instrument, not the product, on two independent legs:**
1. **Calibration** — one known stimulus fired into the live region, pushes counted:
   `probeMultiplier: 4`. Then `4 raw ÷ 4 = 1` announcement. **Steps 8 and 9 re-probe rather
   than carrying the number forward** — measured per step, not assumed.
2. **The single-observer timestamp — decisive alone.** The diagnostic's *one* observer
   logged **exactly one** timestamped record every time. **A real double-announce would have
   logged two.**

⇒ **The product announces each refusal EXACTLY ONCE. No accessibility defect. DD5 intact.**

**The absence claim is untouched**: multiplication **duplicates; it cannot mask**. A
`'Move canceled'` would have appeared **4×** — interleaved or blocked, visible either way.
It appeared **0×**.

**The real masking hazard is the DEDUP, not the multiplier — and this is the sharp part.**
Collapsing *consecutive identical* strings makes **1 real announcement indistinguishable
from 2 identical ones** (2 × 4 = 8 consecutive identical pushes → collapses to ONE),
**reinstating the exact final-value blindness the recorder exists to defeat, one layer up.**
The Executor's `trueSequence` is such a collapse and **must never be cited as "the
announcement sequence"**. **Folded**: the arm disconnects first and calibrates; the two safe
primitives are `rawPushCount ÷ calibrated multiplier` and the timestamped single observer.

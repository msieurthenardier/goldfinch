been**. `src/main/find-overlay-geometry.js` — the pure bounds formula — is

in **three distinct guest widths** — 1398→1007, 1038→647, 2558→2167 — each
landing on the helper's prediction **to the pixel**, plus **exact round-trip
identity** (0px bar-frame diff after panel open→close, maximize→restore, menu

diff would have been confounded by fixture differences; this is cleaner.

## The isVisible-vs-screen limitation (the run's sharpest question, ruled)

The Executor flagged its own honest limitation: the composite draws the overlay
only when `findOverlay.isVisible()` is true — it reflects the **manager's
`visible` flag, not an independent read of the screen**. A defect desyncing
`visible` from real stack presence would be invisible to this observable in both
directions, and `desktopCapturer` (the independent path) is unavailable under
WSLg.

**Validator's ruling: a genuine property of the venue; it does not undercut any
checkpoint in this run.** A scoped pass, not a charitable one. Three grounds:

1. **It does not touch the geometry checkpoints (2/3/4/8) at all.** `isVisible()`
   gates *whether* the layer is drawn — not *where* or *what*. Position comes
   from the real view's `getBounds()`; content from a real `capturePage()` of the
   overlay's webContents. A flag desync could make the bar wrongly present or
   absent; it could not make it appear at x2167 unless the real view's bounds
   were 2167. And the float-not-inset limb reads the **guest's** composited
   position, which never consults the overlay's flag.
2. **It does bite on the presence/absence limb (2-presence, 5, 6, 7)** — those
   prove "`visible` flipped correctly *and* the real overlay content was
   drawn/withheld accordingly", not "the overlay left the physical screen".
   Recorded honestly on those steps.
3. **But it is not in leg 1's blast radius.** Leg 1 changed where overlay state
   *lives* (module-scope → per-instance closure), not `show()`/`hide()`'s
   internals where the flag/stack coupling sits. The Validator checked that
   coupling at source: **both functions are fully synchronous** —
   `cv.addChildView(v); v.setVisible(true); visible = true;` and
   `cv.removeChildView(view); visible = false;` — no `await` between call and
   assignment, so the desync window is nil absent a throw. Meanwhile the
   realistic leg-1 regressions (DD7 hook losing its wiring, DD5 hook not firing,
   the resize observer not calling `setBounds`, `show()` reading a stale/shared
   bounds slot) **all** manifest as a wrong `visible` value or wrong bounds — and
   this observable catches every one.

**The one defect class the venue cannot see is not the class this run exists to
catch.** Debrief carry: record it as a standing venue limitation to be
discharged where `desktopCapturer` works — the macOS landing gate is the natural
home, and the spec already scopes macOS there.

## Spec-quality observations (recommendations only; nothing was edited)

1. **The spec's own caveat is CONFIRMED STALE — and actively harmful.**
   `find-overlay-geometry.md:88-97` says the WSLg fallback "**may not composite
   the overlay view**". That was written pre-F7-DD5 and is **false for this
   build**, superseded by the layer list at `main.js:681-709`. Worse, it
   instructs a future Executor to "defer overlay-presence to the HAT if the
   fallback is in force" — which on this build would **wrongly park a step that
   is fully assertable**. Erratum recommended; the Executor was right not to edit
   a regression baseline mid-run.
2. **Step 8's strengthening is worth promoting — and it is directly on leg 1's
   risk.** Mechanism confirmed at `find-overlay-manager.js:219`:
   `const showBounds = getActiveGuestBounds() || lastGuestBounds;`. The guest
   resized 1398→2558 **while the overlay was hidden**, and the bar still reopened
   at the correct new x2167 rather than its last-shown x1007 — so the live
   per-call fetch was exercised, and the `lastGuestBounds` fallback (which **leg
   1 made per-instance, where it was previously a shared module slot any window
   could stomp**) would have stranded it. This arrived **by accident** via the
   WSLg lag, so it is **not reproducible on demand**. Recommend promoting it into
   the spec as a deliberate assertion: hide find, resize the window, reopen,
   assert the bar lands at the new guest's top-right. That converts a lucky
   observation into a repeatable regression net over exactly the state-ownership
   change leg 1 made.
3. **"Default jar" — harmless naming slip, not a spec bug.** The build's
   fresh-profile default is `personal`; no jar named "Default" exists. Read as
   "the default jar" the intent is satisfied and no assertion depends on the
   name. Lowercase it whenever the spec is next touched.
